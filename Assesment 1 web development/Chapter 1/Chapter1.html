Sinigang

Sinigang is a Filipino dish that has a sour and savory taste. Typically, this food is centered around the meat that is added into the dish. It is considered one of the oldest fishes and is considered one of the best vegetable soups in the country.

This dish includes the following ingridients:

  1. 4¼ cups water
  2. 2 cups beef spare rib cut into chunks
  3. 1 pc red onion, quartered
  4. 2 pcs medium sized tomato, quartered
  5. 8 pcs sitaw, sliced into 2” pieces
  6. 250 g kangkong stalk and leaves
  7. 1 pc eggplant, sliced
  8. 2 pcs chili pangsigang
  9. 1 (20g) pack Knorr Sinigang Sampalok Mix Original
  10. Optional ingridients would be okra and labanos

Here's a step by step guide on how you should prepare this dish!

  1. Combine water and beef ribs in a pressure cooker. Pressure cook for 25 to 30 minutes. Note: You can transfer this later to a larger cooking pot once the beef gets tender, if needed.
  2. Add onion and tomato. Cook for 3 minutes.
  3. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix. Stir.
  4. Put the daikon radish (labanos) into the pot. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add long green pepper, okra, snake beans, eggplant, and the kangkong stalks. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Season with patis and ground black pepper.
  7. Add kangkong leaves. Cover the pot and turn the stovetop off. Let it stay for 5 minutes.
  8. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
  9. Share and enjoy!
